About us.

What is the Foundation?

The Change.org Foundation is a global non-profit operating in a diverse set of countries with very different realities, spanning Latin America, Asia, Europe, and soon, Africa.

In many of these countries, democracy is broken -- people in power are not accountable to the people whose lives they impact. We want there to be a world where decision-making spaces are open, so that people can fully participate in holding the powerful to account.

We believe the only path to sustainable positive social change is change led by the people themselves, and that the power of technology can be harnessed to enable more people to be a part of decision making. That’s why we leverage the global Change.org platform and also deliver highly localized programs and technology that accelerate citizen leadership and collective action on critical issues and in critical moments.

2-line description

The Change.org Foundation is a global non-profit that leverages the global Change.org platform in 11 countries, combining it with highly localized programs, analytics intelligence and technology for collective action.

Mission

We share a mission with the Change.org family: empower people everywhere to create the change they want to see.

Vision

By the end of 2021, the Foundation will serve users across four continents as a sustainable, resilient, and unified global organization. Every country operation will be sustained by users who deeply believe in the power of Change.org campaigns and citizen-led collective action and we partner with mission-aligned funders to deliver high-impact, relevant local programs.

As a network of countries facing diverse realities, our strategies and systems balance local flexibility and entrepreneurialism with a shared commitment to building the most globally recognizable and powerful social change brand in the world. Our culture celebrates both diversity and global solidarity; we exercise servant leadership at every level; and we build our organization in a way that honors the courage and tenacity of our users, members, and partners working on the frontlines of social change.

Our principles.

The Foundation adopted the values of the Change.org family and in addition developed a set of principles under which we commit to work.

We have a grassroots mindset.

Our internal decisions and strategies are based on deep listening of our staff and users' experience. All of the Foundation's work is focussed on serving the realisation of the mission in our countries and our external projects are true collaborations with users and partners.

We thrive together.

We believe in our collective impact working together as a Foundation. We actively support each other in our learning. We ask questions that go beyond the platform and we make time to reflect and share with our colleagues.

We are impact-driven

As a non-profit, we will always prioritise maximising impact. Wherever possible, we design our work so that the products and value it creates are free, open and commons-based, creating a shared environment for social good.

We are free to create.

Fostering new ideas for empowering our users and petition starters is an everyday challenge that every member of staff owns. We are free to decide our strategies and we will be supported to try new things, learning from failures as well as successes.

We are diverse, inclusive and transparent.

We celebrate the diversity of our countries, staff and users within the Foundation. We are all building an empowering place to work, so that all Foundation staff will be involved in the decisions that affect them. Our users and colleagues know who we are and what we are working on, because we are proactive and transparent in our communications.

Logo & Design.

How to use our logo?

Our brand plays a role in the lives of many people and in broader societies. Our logo is recognized by millions around the world; it's what unites our actions, platforms and products with the Change.org ecosystem. Whenever we design or create something new, we should make sure that it follows our brand guidelines.

 The logo should always be used in Change.org's red and dark gray for the country name.

 If you need to use the logo with a dark background, use the white version.

 If you need to use our logo in text, the “C” and the first letter of the country name should always be capitalized.

Wrong ways of using our Logo.

 Do not use the Change.org icon and logo at the same time.

 Do not change the logo color.

 Do not stretch or alter the logo.

 Do not use the Foundation logo next to the Change.org icon.

 Do not apply effects or stylize the logo.

 Do not split the logo into two lines.

 Use icons from Flaticon's add-on for G Suite

If you need more icons to make your presentation even more awesome you can use Flaticon's add-on for Google Slides. Here a quick tutorial on how to install it.

Use icons from Flaticon's add-on for G Suite

1. Open your Google Slides presentation

2. Click Add-ons on the top bar → then click Get add-ons.

3. Locate Flaticon extension and install it by giving account permission.

4. Run the extension. Click add-ons → Icons for slides & docs → Start

5. Serch for icons, choose size and color and insert them to the presentation. IMPORTANT: Make sure you only use icons relevant to our brand style.

Enjoy!

Voice & tone

The voice of the Change.org Foundation is consistent across all channels.
Our personality is...

   Hopeful

   Credible

   Approachable

   Innovative

Our voice is human. It’s a familiar and friendly voice that speaks with clarity and certainty. We like telling stories about people who share our vision at Change.org and are committed to giving you good news and feel-good updates about what our global community is doing to impact positive change. We have good stories to share and we’re ready to show you the numbers to back them up.
Our voice is...

  • Conversational but not too informal
  • Confident but not arrogant
  • Intelligent but not technical
  • Expert but not bossy
  • Mature but not intimidating
  • Professional but not stodgy
  • Wholesome but not traditional
  • Light-hearted but not playful
  • Helpful but not overbearing
  • Receptive (open to dialogue) but not swayable
