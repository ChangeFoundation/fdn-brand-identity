Writing guide.

Words are powerful tools that reflect our brand in the product as well as in external communication. The grammar and style rules here were developed with American English in mind. Other countries should try to adhere to similar, unified principles, but should feel free to adapt to languages and dialects as needed.

Key writing principles
  • Paragraphs should be broken down into short, scannable chunks, ideally no more than three sentences.
  • Use bullet points, when applicable.
  • Don’t use jargon or acronyms that people won’t understand.
  • Focus on the message you’re wanting to communicate.
  • Consider the audience you’re writing for and what their needs are.
  • Cut out any extraneous information that isn’t needed to understand your core message.
Presentation tips

Tell a story: Think about your main message, structure, and its supporting points first, then create your slides using that story as the outline. A strong, focused message will help prevent your presentation from veering off into complex, superfluous topics.

With text, less is more: If there are a lot of words on a slide, you’re asking your audience to split their attention between what they’re reading and what they’re hearing or comprehending. What content will add clarity to your argument without complicating the message?

One idea per slide: tell your story over several slides instead of cramming everything onto a few slides. Ask yourself: what are the main points you wish to make? Are these points structured in a logical, coherent way?

Voice and tone

Our voice is open

We provide a platform for people who want to change issues they care about, regardless of background or ideology. Write with a voice that’s neutral and wouldn’t alienate people with differing perspectives.

Our voice is suportive

Building an effective campaign can often feel like an overwhelming and emotional process. Write with a voice that’s clear, helpful, and offers guidance without being prescriptive.

Our voice is empowering

No matter how passionate a person is about an issue, it can be difficult to actually change something. Write with a voice that motivates people and encourages them to keep pushing forward, while respecting the gravity of their cause.

The tone fluctuates depending on what’s being communicated and how the message is delivered. For example, the tone should be warm and friendly when we’re welcoming people to Change.org, but serious and straightforward when entering credit card information or when encountering a site error.
